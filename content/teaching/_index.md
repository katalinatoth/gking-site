### [Gov 2020: The Hidden Curriculum](gov2020/)
**Fall 2025**

Gov 2020 has two components: A popular semester-long project where students learn how to write a publishable scholarly paper, and a set of lectures and sections where they learn from other researchers' experiences. The syllabus covers classes, lectures, and sections for instructors and assignments for students.

---

### [Math Prefresher for Political Scientists](math-prefresher/)
**2023** (Faculty advisor)

Not only do the quantitative and formal modeling courses at Harvard require mathematics and computer programming -- it's becoming increasingly difficult to take courses in political economy, American politics, comparative politics, or international relations without a solid math foundation.

---

### [Quantitative Social Science Methods, I: Government 2001, and E-200](gov2001/)
**Fall 2022**

Gov2001 is the first (or sometimes second) course for incoming Harvard Government Department PhD students; also taken by graduate and undergraduate students in other departments, and students elsewhere through the Harvard Extension School as Stat E-200.

---

### [Workshop in Applied Statistics](workshop-applied-statistics/)
**2017**

The Applied Statistics Workshop (Gov 3009) meets all academic year, Wednesdays, 12pm-1:30pm. This workshop is a forum for faculty, graduate students, visiting scholars, and others in the area to present and discuss methodological and theoretical work in progress.

---

### [Strategies of Political Inquiry, Government 2010](gov2010/)
**2010**

Gary King, Robert Putnam, and Sidney Verba. If you could learn only one thing in graduate school, it should be how to do scholarly research. You should be able to assess the state of a scholarly literature, identify interesting questions, and choose appropriate research strategies.

---

### [Introduction to Quantitative Political Methodology, G1000](g1000/)
**2010**

Introduction to major quantitative techniques used in political science. Covers exploratory data analysis, as well as descriptive and causal inference.

---

### [Designing Political Inquiry, Government 1003 (for undergrads)](gov1003/)

An undergraduate course on designing political research.
