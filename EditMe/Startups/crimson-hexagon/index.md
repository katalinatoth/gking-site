  <p style="font-style:italic;color:#666">The Story of Crimson Hexagon &mdash; Originally posted <a href="https://twitter.com/kinggary/status/1368935083919372292" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@kinggary on Twitter</a>, 8 March 2021</p>
  <p>A little more on the big news: Crimson Hexagon, the company I founded with Candace Fleming in 2007 (now called @Brandwatch after a merger), is being acquired by @Cision in the biggest exit ever in social media measurement; see <a href="/press-cision-brandwatch/">brandwatch.com/blog/cision</a>. 1/10</p>
  <p>Our founding principle: The invention of social media made it possible for one person to speak &amp; potentially for billions to hear, but no technology had made it possible for any one person to understand what so many others were saying. Our technology does. 2/10</p>
  <p>Over time I've learned: Industry can hire some people we can't attract to the university. I can hire some types that industry would never be able to lure away from academia. The key is working together: a flow of information back and forth between sectors makes both better. 3/10</p>
  <p>Crimson Hexagon began with unrelated <a href="https://gking.harvard.edu/publication/verbal-autopsy-methods-with-multiple-causes-of-death/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">research</a> with my postdoc Ying Lu (now at NYU) that enabled WHO to estimate causes of death in developing countries that have no infrastructure for death certifications. 4/10</p>
  <p>I was studying political views in social media (the "blogosphere"!) with my grad student Dan Hopkins, but all NLP methods failed miserably. <a href="https://gking.harvard.edu/publication/a-method-of-automated-nonparametric-content-analysis-for-social-science/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">We finally discovered</a> that adapting the same cause-of-death methods solved the academic problem, and also <a href="/press-otd-crimson-hexagon/">led to founding this startup</a>. 5/10</p>
  <p>There've been SO many academic articles (and knowledge for public good) that wouldn't exist without this industry connection. The academic benefits of leveraging the commercial world are massively underestimated in the academy. The reverse is surely true as well. 6/10</p>
  <p>This also made possible research <a href="https://gking.harvard.edu/#chinese-censorship" target="_blank" rel="noopener">reverse engineering Chinese censorship &amp; fabrication of social media posts</a>, the remarkable <a href="https://gking.harvard.edu/publication/how-the-news-media-activate-public-expression-and-influence-national-agendas/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">effects of small media outlets</a> on the US national conversation, &amp; many others. 7/10</p>
  <p>One more: I'd been trying to improve our Crimson Hexagon algorithm for years when my grad students, Connor Jersak &amp; Anton Strezhnev, &amp; I finally <a href="https://gking.harvard.edu/publication/an-improved-method-of-automated-nonparametric-content-analysis-for-social-science/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">solved the problem</a> (via an intergenerational breakthrough: Anton was previously Dan Hopkins' undergrad!). 8/10</p>
  <p>Congratulations to @Cision; big things to come for sure! See <a href="/press-cision-brandwatch/">Cision Press Release</a>. 9/10</p>
  <p>Thanks to my coauthors; cofounder; CEOs Candace Fleming, @scootr, @triciagottesman, @StephanieSNewby, &amp; @joodoo9; &gt;500 Brandwatch employees; thousands elsewhere whose jobs depend on Brandwatch software; investors; &amp; Harvard's OTD, IQSS, Gov Dept, FAS, OPP, OGC, IRB, etc. 10/10</p>
